Implications & Policy Recommendations

Income-Based Energy Efficiency Impact

Low-Income Areas:
  • Face issues with poor housing quality and low energy efficiency
  • Limited potential for improvement due to infrastructure constraints

Recommended Interventions:

  • Energy subsidies and zero-rate loans
  • Energy vouchers based on family size
  • Targeted insulation and heating system upgrades
High-Income Areas:

Policy Recommendations:

  • Tax credits for large-scale building retrofits
  • Incentives for sustainable energy solutions
  • Support for high-end energy efficiency investments

Source: Department for Business, Energy & Industrial Strategy, 2022

Monitoring and Implementation Strategies

Impact monitoring should utilize:

  • Energy intensity indicators
  • Energy security metrics
  • Real-time monitoring technologies
  • Big data analytics

Based on Filippini et al., 2014

Seasonal Energy Consumption Patterns

Key Seasons:

  • Heating season (winter)
  • Cooling season (summer)
  • Transition seasons (spring/fall)
  • Extreme weather periods

Season-Specific Recommendations:

  • Heating subsidies for cold regions
  • Promotion of green building designs
  • Support for passive cooling technologies
  • Targeted assistance for larger households

Future Research Priorities

  • Focus on electricity usage patterns
  • Investigation of multimodal distribution in electricity data
  • Analysis of energy-efficient appliance adoption
  • Long-term monitoring of efficiency improvements

Conclusions

This study employed a linear regression model to analyze the relationships between taxpayer income, household energy efficiency ratings, and household size with natural gas consumption.

Key Findings:
  • Higher-income areas show increased natural gas consumption
  • Larger household sizes correlate with higher consumption
  • Energy efficiency improvements significantly reduce gas use
  • Income and household size have interactive effects on consumption
  • Energy efficiency improvements have varying impacts based on income levels
Study Limitations:
  • Reliance on district-level aggregate data
  • Exclusion of electricity consumption analysis
  • Unaccounted factors in housing type and appliance usage

Moving Forward

Future policies should prioritize:

  • Targeted interventions based on income levels
  • Comprehensive monitoring systems
  • Integration of seasonal consumption patterns
  • Household size considerations in policy design
