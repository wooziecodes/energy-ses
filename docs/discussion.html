Discussion & Limitations

Borough-Level Data Limitations

The model is limited by the use of borough level data, due to a lack of household level data for gas consumption. This aggregation makes it challenging to develop effective policy recommendations for decreasing gas consumption.

Understanding household-level relationships is crucial as existing literature shows that household income has a substantial effect on household energy consumption (Gassar, Yun and Kim, 2019, p.7).

We recommend:

  • Collection of household level data for income level
  • Direct measurement of electricity and gas consumption
  • Avoiding reliance on survey data

Lack of Electricity Consumption Model

The absence of a regression model for electricity consumption limits our understanding of potential differences in consumption patterns between gas and electricity.

"Per capita electricity consumption is ... more sensitive to changes in income than gas consumption" (Brounen, Kok and Quigley, 2012, p.932)

Future Research Direction

This area requires investigation by experienced data scientists to develop a more comprehensive understanding of energy consumption patterns.

Household Count Control

The model didn't control for the number of households per borough, potentially causing inflated predictions for mean gas consumption. Boroughs with fewer households may have disproportionate influence on predicted values.

Future studies should incorporate controls for the number of households per borough to improve prediction accuracy.

Appliance Choice Considerations

While household appliance choices significantly impact energy consumption, data limitations prevented controlling for this variable (Brounen, Kok and Quigley, 2012, p.941).

Future Research Direction

Investigation needed into how household income levels influence appliance choices and subsequent energy consumption (Steemers and Yun, 2009).

Building Type Variations

The model didn't account for different types of residential buildings, despite evidence that building type affects household energy consumption (Mohajeri et al., 2023, pp.2-3).

Detached buildings show highest consumption for both gas and electricity (Mohajeri et al., 2023).

Future research should:

  • Control for different residence types
  • Consider borough-specific building type distributions
  • Account for both building characteristics and occupancy profiles (Mohajeri et al., 2023, p.4)
