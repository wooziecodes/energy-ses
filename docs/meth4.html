Practical Implications

Income Effects

Higher income boroughs show increased gas consumption, but this effect is moderated by both household size and energy efficiency. This suggests that energy efficiency improvements could help mitigate income-related increases in consumption.

Energy Efficiency Impact

The strong negative coefficient for energy ratings (-333.53) indicates that improvements in energy efficiency could substantially reduce gas consumption, particularly in higher-income areas.

Household Size Considerations

The large positive coefficient for household size (6,858) suggests that policies targeting household energy efficiency might be particularly important in areas with larger average household sizes.

Key Takeaway

While higher income is associated with increased gas consumption, this relationship is significantly moderated by both household size and energy efficiency. This suggests that energy efficiency improvements could be an effective way to reduce consumption, particularly in higher-income areas with larger households.
